Kiran Abbavaram

Early life

Kiran Abbavaram was born on 15 July 1992[1][2] in Rayachoti, Andhra Pradesh.[3] He is a B. Tech graduate and worked as a network consultant for 2.5 years in Chennai and Bangalore. He started doing short films while working and later left his job to pursue a full-time career in films. One of his short films, Sreekaram, was remade into a feature film with the same name in 2021.[4] He got engaged to his first co-actor Rahasya Gorak on 13 March 2024.[5]

Kiran Abbavaram made his debut with Raja Vaaru Rani Gaaru (2019). Sangeetha Devi of The Hindu in her review stated: "Abbavaram shows he is no longer a mere YouTube or a short film sensation — he has the looks and promises to be a part of good stories and movies".[6] He got greater recognition and appreciation with his second film SR Kalyanamandapam, which he also wrote.[7] The film received positive reviews from the critics and was a box office success.[8][9]

Career

In 2022, Abbavaram had two releases—Sebastian P.C. 524 and Sammathame.[10][11] Murali Krishna CH of The New Indian Express called Sebastian a half-hearted drama but opined Abbavaram's performance was its biggest strength.[12] In 2023 Abbavaram had two releases one Meter and another is Rules Ranjann. Meter was panned badly[13] and Rules Ranjann was an average fare[14] with critics praising it for its comedic elements. In 2024, Abbavaram’s upcoming film "Dil Ruba" is directed by Viswa Karun. The female lead is Rukshar Dhillon. Praveen K. L. will edit the film, and the music will be composed by Sam C. S..[15]
